## Potential Actions

### Featured: 524-526 Vallejo St CUA Appeal — Board of Supervisors, Tue 4/7, 2 PM

**What's happening:** The owners of a four-unit residential building on Vallejo Street near Telegraph Hill want to legalize merging three upper-floor units into one and reinstate a ground-floor unit. The Planning Commission let the application expire without acting (a "de facto denial"). The owners appealed to the Board, which votes Tuesday.

**Why you'd care:** This is a unit-merger case in the Telegraph Hill-North Beach SUD — it trades three smaller units for one large one plus one restored unit, netting minus-two units in a rent-controlled building. The outcome sets a signal for how the Board handles unit mergers in D3's older housing stock.

**What you can do:** Public comment is open at the Board meeting (Tue 4/7, 2 PM, City Hall Room 250). You can also email the Board clerk: board.of.supervisors@sfgov.org. Reference file 260021.

## Citywide

**Budget & Policy.** SF's budget deficit is on track to shrink by $300M, per the city controller. At the same time, Mayor Lurie is pushing to cut the CEO/executive gross receipts tax — the same tax that helped close prior deficits. The tension: cutting it now could reopen the hole later.

**Surveillance votes.** Three surveillance technology policies hit the Board this week: police electronic location tracking (Tue), DPW drones and ALPR cameras for illegal dumping (Tue), and Elections security cameras (Rules, Mon). These are approval votes under SF's surveillance oversight ordinance.

**Congressional race.** Candidates for the open CA-11/SF seat debated this week. Wiener, Chan, and Chakrabarti went on the attack. Chakrabarti's campaign is deploying 250+ canvassers at up to $45/hr.

**Obituaries.** John Elberling, TODCO leader and SoMa housing/political power player, died at 79. Rod Diridon, Bay Area transit leader and high-speed rail champion, died at 87. Both shaped regional housing and transportation policy for decades.

**Sacks/GrowSF.** Trump advisor David Sacks asked for his donation back from GrowSF, the Lurie-allied political group.

**Asian Law Caucus at SCOTUS.** SF's Asian Law Caucus is at the center of the Supreme Court birthright citizenship case — a fight with deep roots in the city's own history.

**Federal funding.** SF trans orgs facing funding uncertainty as federal cuts ripple through local nonprofits. SF leaders passed a new local law after the Supreme Court lifted the federal ban on conversion therapy.

**14 landmark designations** move through Land Use Monday — Bob Ross House, Maud's, Castro Rock Steam Baths, SF AIDS Foundation, and 10 others, all in D8/D9. Preservation continues to outpace new construction in those corridors.

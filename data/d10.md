## Potential Actions

### Board of Supervisors votes on surveillance policies -- Tue 4/7, 2:00 PM

**What's happening:** Three surveillance technology policies are up for final passage at the full Board: police electronic location tracking devices, DPW drone use, and DPW cameras with automatic license plate readers for illegal dumping. A fourth (Department of Elections security cameras) is in committee Monday at Rules.

**Why you'd care:** These policies set the rules for how city agencies can use tracking and imaging tech on public streets -- including in D10 neighborhoods. The electronic tracking policy governs how SFPD uses GPS and cell-site data. The DPW camera policy authorizes license plate readers specifically for dumping enforcement, which has been a persistent issue along Bayview corridors.

**What you can do:** Public comment is open at the Board meeting, Tue 4/7 at 2:00 PM, City Hall Room 250. If you can't attend, a 2-minute email to the full Board (board.of.supervisors@sfgov.org) counts -- reference File Nos. 250979 (police tracking), 260287 (DPW drones/cameras). ## Permits & Development

Fourteen demolition permits were filed April 3 across Block 4167 (Potrero Hill -- Missouri St, Turner Tr, Watchman Wy), targeting two-story residential buildings totaling roughly 80 units. All filings are on a single parcel, suggesting a coordinated redevelopment project clearing multiple structures at once.

Other notable permits: a new two-story light industrial building at 1723-1727 Wallace Ave (Bayview), and the $1.5M conversion of a church at 1221 Oakdale Ave into a childcare center.

A market at 1999 Jerrold Ave filed for demolition.

## Housing Pipeline

The big story at 249 Pennsylvania Ave: the previous 92-unit market-rate project (via State Density Bonus) was withdrawn, and a new 124-unit, 100% affordable family housing project filed under the same program. The new building would be 9 stories with units at 30-50% AMI, including family daycare and supportive services. Separately, 47 units proposed at 3275-3333 San Bruno Ave across three buildings, and the 300 De Haro project modified its approval to go fully affordable at 425 units.

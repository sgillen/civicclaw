## Potential Actions

### ⭐ Featured: Board of Supervisors — Surveillance Tech Votes, Tue April 7, 2 PM

**What's happening:** Four separate surveillance technology policies hit the full Board in one meeting: DPW drone and ALPR (automated license plate reader) policies, police electronic location tracking, and Airport Commission surveillance tech for the TNC virtual queue. These are formal adoption votes, not study sessions.

**Why you'd care:** D6 is where most of this technology gets used. SoMa and the Tenderloin have the highest police presence, the most cameras, the most encampment enforcement. Whether you think surveillance tools improve safety or enable overreach, the policies adopted Tuesday will set the rules for how DPW and SFPD use drones, plate readers, and location tracking in your neighborhood. There's also a separate appeal on 524-526 Vallejo St (CUA) on the same agenda.

**What you can do:** Public comment is taken at the meeting — City Hall, Room 250, 2 PM Tuesday. You can also email the Board clerk (board.of.supervisors@sfgov.org) before the meeting with "Public Comment" in the subject line. Reference the specific policy you want to address.

## John Elberling (1946–2026)

John Elberling died March 30 at 79. He ran TODCO — Tenants and Owners Development Corporation — for decades, turning it into the largest affordable housing developer in SoMa. The Yerba Buena redevelopment fights of the 1970s created TODCO, and Elberling turned that origin into thousands of affordable units across SoMa. Whether you live in a TODCO building or just live in a SoMa where affordable housing exists at all, his fingerprints are on it. Mission Local and 48 Hills both have obituaries worth reading.

## Office Renovation Surge

$6.9M+ in commercial tenant improvement permits filed across SoMa and the Financial District in the last week:

- **680 Folsom St** — $2.75M, floors 6-7 full demo and rebuild
- **199 Fremont St** — $1.5M, interior demo across 10 floors (building was unoccupied)
- **555 Mission St** — $1.2M, 27th floor office buildout
- **88 Howard St** — $450K, residential lobby renovation
- **535 Mission St** — $400K, deferred MEP on 6th floor
- **55 Spear St** — $382K, demo floors 30-31
- **303 2nd St** — $250K, 5th floor tenant improvement
- **595 Market St** — $200K, 12th floor suite renovation

The pattern: landlords are spending real money to gut and rebuild unoccupied floors, not just waiting for tenants to show up. 199 Fremont demoing 10 empty floors and 680 Folsom doing a full two-floor reconstruction are not cosmetic refreshes. SoMa office space is being repositioned, not abandoned.

**Other permits of note:**
- **760 Bryant St** — change of use from auto repair to ambulance service ($75K)
- **180 Townsend St** — new illuminated "Andreessen Horowitz" sign ($15K). A16z marking territory.
- **555 9th St** — $400K back-of-house expansion

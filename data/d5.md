## Potential Actions

### ⭐ Featured: SFMTA Engineering Hearing — Thu April 3, 10am

**What's happening:** Two D5 items.

**Item 3 — Stanyan Street RPP expansion:** The east side of Stanyan between Page and Haight (650/670-672 Stanyan only) would become eligible for Area J residential parking permits. No signs yet — eligibility only, requires a separate SFMTA Board vote to activate. Contact: lulu.brien@sfmta.com

**Items 11a–d — Broderick/Oak metering:** Broderick Street between Fell and Oak, and Oak between Broderick and Divisadero, would flip from 2-hour/Area Q permit parking to general metered parking — 4-hour limit, 9am–6pm Mon–Sat. This is the block immediately adjacent to the 400 Divisadero site. SFMTA's rationale is improving turnover and availability.

**Why you'd care:** Broderick/Fell feeds into the Wiggle. The metering question is genuinely ambiguous from a pedestrian/cycling lens — metered turnover can reduce circling, or it can increase it if demand is high. The more interesting angle: this is the block that will be under construction when 400 Divisadero eventually breaks ground. Worth knowing how the curb is configured going in. Contact: kenya.wheeler@sfmta.com, reference Item 11 / Order #6991.

**How to comment:** Join at sfmta.com/EngHearing when your item is called, or submit written comment in advance. "Public Hearing" in subject line. You can also just email before Thursday.

- 618 Octavia — 40 units, State Density Bonus, under review

- 450 O'Farrell — 261 units, major revision, under review since 2013
- 1355 Fulton — 66 units, State Density Bonus, **under review since 2018.** Eight years.

**Approved, not building:**
- 400 Divisadero — 203 units, AB 2011 + State Density Bonus, approval letter December 2024. 15+ months. No permits. No construction. 4Terra Investments has not filed anything. This got 2,700 upvotes on r/SF when it was approved. Nobody's checked in since.

**Cancelled:**
- 105 Turk — 112 units, cancelled March 28. Gone.
- 819 Ellis — 120 units, cancelled.
- 841 Polk — 22 units, cancelled.

The pattern: projects that get approved under streamlined pathways still don't become buildings. The gap between "approval letter issued" and "permits filed" is where D5 housing actually dies. 880 McAllister has been approved for nearly a year. 400 Divisadero for 15 months. Nobody is asking 4Terra what's happening.

## Displacement Watch (New This Week)

6 eviction notices filed in D5 in the last 14 days — flat compared to prior period. But the type breakdown matters:

**1 Ellis Act eviction — 100 block of Webster Street, Hayes Valley.** No Ellis filings in the prior period. Ellis Act = landlord withdrawing the building from the rental market entirely. This is the displacement signal worth tracking — it removes rent-controlled units permanently. One filing isn't a trend, but it's the kind of thing to watch accumulate.

4 other notices (2 breach, 2 nuisance) are on pace. Nuisance filings are actually down 50% from prior period.

## The Sheryl Davis Story

Former Human Rights Commission director Sheryl Davis was arrested Monday alongside James Spingola, described as a nonprofit collaborator she lived with. Both charged with felonies. This is an extension of the corruption probe that led to her ouster last year — Davis ran HRC for years and distributed millions in city contracts. Mayor Breed was not implicated.

The HRC scandal is the clearest example this cycle of the city's nonprofit contracting system operating as a patronage network. Davis steered contracts to organizations connected to Spingola. The question nobody's asking yet: how many other department heads ran similar arrangements, and what's the city's liability exposure? The Ethics Commission lobbying data doesn't capture this because it wasn't lobbying — it was procurement.

## SFCTA: The Transit Funding Agency You Should Know

The SF County Transportation Authority Board meets April 14 at City Hall. They control the Prop L half-cent sales tax, make congestion pricing decisions, and fund Muni Forward bike/ped improvements. Their budget decisions are upstream of a lot of what SFMTA can actually build.

No agenda posted yet for April 14 — but this is the body that will eventually be relevant for any Divisadero corridor infrastructure work. Worth knowing they exist before you need them.

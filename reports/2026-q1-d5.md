## Potential Actions

### 🎯 Featured: Comment on Wiggle Corridor Metering

**What's happening:** SFMTA Engineering Hearing on April 3 will vote on installing meters on Broderick Street between Fell and Oak — right on the Wiggle bike route. This converts free 2-hour parking to paid 4-hour metered parking, 9am-6pm Mon-Sat.

**Why you'd care:** The stated goal is "parking availability and turnover." But Broderick is the main north-south connector for the Wiggle — you ride it every time you bike from the Panhandle to Lower Haight. Metering affects traffic patterns, which affects bike safety. The adjacent Oak Street segment (Broderick to Divisadero) is also getting meters.

## Housing: The 400 Divisadero Watch Continues

**400 Divisadero** (203 units, AB 2011 + State Density Bonus) received its approval letter December 23, 2024. Three months into 2026: no building permit application. Developer 4Terra Investments remains silent.

For context: typical market-rate projects take 12-36 months from approval to permit filing due to financing challenges. 400 Divisadero is still within normal range — but the clock is ticking. If no permit by end of 2026, it's likely dead or frozen.

**New approval this quarter:**
- **650 Divisadero** — 107 units, 100% affordable. Approval letter issued. Developer: Grove Street Development LP with MOHCD. This one should move faster — affordable projects have pre-assembled financing. The Divisadero corridor could see two major projects break ground in 2026-2027... or neither.

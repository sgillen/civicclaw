# District 5 — 2025 Annual Summary

*Coverage: Housing pipeline, building permits, evictions from Socrata historical data. Board/commission actions limited to available records.*

---

## TLDR

- **Housing pipeline stalled badly** — 3 major projects totaling 254 units cancelled (39 Taylor, 819 Ellis, 841 Polk). Only 400 Divisadero (203 units, AB 2011) moved to approval.
- **Eviction notices doubled** — 284 total in D5 over the year, up 104% from prior period. Non-payment (+228%) and nuisance (+53%) drove the surge. Tenderloin bore 61% of notices.
- **The Tenderloin crisis deepened** — eviction concentration, 311 complaint volume, and permit activity all show the neighborhood under severe stress.
- **7 displacement-signal evictions** — 6 owner move-in, 1 Ellis Act (100 block Webster, Hayes Valley, March 2026). Low overall but a slight uptick.
- **Building permit volume steady** — 707 permits filed in D5, 110 high-tier. Significant: 2245-2255 Post St philanthropic facility ($82M total across two permits), 1439 Oak St ADU conversion.

## Housing Pipeline: More Cancelled Than Built

D5's housing pipeline ran backwards in 2025. The streamlined approval programs (AB 2011, SB 35, State Density Bonus) that were supposed to accelerate construction instead became a graveyard of good intentions.

**Cancelled projects (2025):**
- **39 Taylor St** — 112 units, State Density Bonus. Cancelled.
- **819 Ellis St** — 120 units, State Density Bonus. Cancelled.
- **841 Polk St** — 22 units, State Density Bonus. Cancelled.

**Total cancelled: 254 units.** That's more than an entire affordable housing building, gone.

**Approved but stuck:**
- **400 Divisadero** — 203 units, AB 2011 + Density Bonus. Approval letter issued Dec 2024. **No building permits filed 15 months later.** Developer: 4Terra Investments. This is D5's flagship streamlined project. It should be under construction by now. It isn't.
- **880 McAllister** — 91 units. Approved. No construction visible.
- **540 Turk** — 88 units. Approved. Sitting.
- **550 O'Farrell** — 140 units. **On hold.** Explicitly paused.

**Currently under review:**
- **468 Turk** — 85-101 units (two filings). Still processing.
- **1355 Fulton** — 66 units. Under review.
- **618 Octavia** — 36 units. Pending review.

The pattern is clear: approvals are happening, but nothing is getting built. The 4-5 year approval-to-move-in timeline documented in the build references isn't even starting — projects are dying in the "approval to permit" dead zone or going on hold before construction financing can close.

## Evictions: The Tenderloin Is Hemorrhaging

284 eviction notices filed in D5 over the year — more than double the prior period's 139.

**By neighborhood:**
| Neighborhood | Count | Share |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| Tenderloin | 174 | 61% |
| Western Addition | 37 | 13% |
| Hayes Valley | 28 | 10% |
| Haight Ashbury | 20 | 7% |
| Japantown | 12 | 4% |

The Tenderloin isn't just the largest neighborhood in D5 — it's bearing a disproportionate share of the district's housing instability.

**By type (year-over-year change):**
- Non-payment: 82 notices (+228%)
- Nuisance: 98 notices (+53%)
- Breach: 76 notices (+162%)
- Owner move-in: 6 notices (+50%) — *displacement signal*
- Capital improvement: 8 notices (+60%)
- Ellis Act: 1 notice (flat) — *displacement signal*

The non-payment surge (+228%) is the headline, but it's the *nuisance* category that's interesting — 98 notices, nearly triple the prior period's pace. That could be landlords using nuisance clauses more aggressively, or it could be genuine behavior issues in SROs. Either way, it's a leading indicator of tenant instability.

**Displacement-signal evictions** (Ellis Act + Owner Move-In) remained low at 7 total — but that's 7 units permanently removed from the rental market.

## Building Permits: Maintenance Mode

707 building permits filed in D5 across the year. The breakdown:
- High-tier: 110
- Medium-tier: 501
- Low-tier: 96

**Notable permits:**
- **2245-2255 Post St** — Two permits totaling $82M for a "philanthropic facility" (appears to be JFCS expansion). This is new construction at scale.
- **1439 Oak St** — $527K ADU/alteration project. Site work + new unit.
- **1530 Haight St** — $437K selective demo and renovation.
- **424 Ellis St** — $500K remodel of 5 units (kitchens + bathrooms).

Most of the 707 permits are maintenance — reroofing, window replacement, minor alterations. The district isn't seeing significant new residential construction despite the housing crisis.

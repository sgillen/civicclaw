## Potential Actions

### ⭐ Featured: SFMTA Broderick/Oak Metering — Hearing Thursday April 3

**What's happening:** Items 11a–d would convert the RPP spots on Broderick Street (both sides, between Fell and Oak) and the Oak Street north side (between Broderick and Divisadero) to 4-hour metered parking, 9am–6pm Mon–Sat. These are the streets immediately adjacent to 400 Divisadero — the 203-unit AB 2011 project.

**Why you'd care (car-free lens):** This is about how the Divisadero corridor functions for people on foot and bike, not about parking for drivers. Metered parking on that block changes turnover patterns — whether it helps or hurts pedestrian activity depends on what replaces the current underutilized RPP spots. The metering request was initiated by SFMTA staff to address "parking availability and turnover issues." If you want the Divisadero corridor to be a walkable destination rather than a cut-through, this hearing is worth watching.

**What you can do:** The hearing is Thursday, April 3 at 10am. You can comment in writing to Kenya.wheeler@sfmta.com with "Public Hearing" in the subject line, or join online at sfmta.com/EngHearing when the item is called. Items 11a–d are the Broderick/Oak items. Also: item 3 (Stanyan RPP extension, Page to Haight) is D5-adjacent.

## The Landmark Blitz Continues

Thirty-three landmark initiations in one Board meeting on March 24. D5 properties directly in the batch:
- **2035–2047 Fillmore Street** (Upper Fillmore Storefronts)
- **2049–2051 Fillmore Street** (Lincoln Grill)
- **1335–1337 Sutter Street** (Congregation Emanu-El Religious School / Grabhorn Press)
- **2015 Steiner Street** (Pacific States Telephone Co / National Urban League)
- **2402 Steiner Street** (Arthur Castle Home)

That's five D5 properties in one meeting. Once a landmark initiation passes, HPC handles the actual designation hearing — typically 3–6 months later. Each designation adds preservation constraints that make demolition or major alteration harder. Mission Local ran a piece this week: *"Is historic preservation in San Francisco in a state of decay?"* — worth a read for the full framing debate.

The Board is moving fast. Q1 2026 is on pace for 100+ initiations. Whether this is clearing a legitimate backlog or systematically tightening constraints on the Fillmore corridor is the question nobody at City Hall has explicitly answered.

## Broderick/Oak + 400 Divisadero: The Thread

The SFMTA hearing items (11a–d) cover Broderick Street between Fell and Oak, and Oak between Broderick and Divisadero. That's literally the block face adjacent to 400 Divisadero's parcel (Block 1216). 

4Terra Investments got their approval letter in December 2024. Fifteen months later: no building permits, no construction timeline public, no press coverage since Mission Local's October 2024 article. The metering proposal is SFMTA staff-initiated, not developer-driven. But the timing is notable — infrastructure changes typically happen in the lead-up to construction activity, not years in advance. Worth watching whether permit applications appear in April-May.

## Lucky Grocery at 1750 Fulton

The Board passed a resolution urging Save Mart Companies to reconsider closing the Lucky supermarket at 1750 Fulton Street (Western Addition) on September 11, 2026. This is a food access issue for a neighborhood that already has limited grocery options. No binding power here — it's a symbolic resolution. But it signals the Board sees the closure as a problem worth flagging publicly.

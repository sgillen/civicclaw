## The Fielder Crisis

The biggest D9 story right now has nothing to do with policy. Supervisor Jackie Fielder checked into a hospital around mid-March and was absent from City Hall for roughly two weeks before the news became public on March 28.

She initially told Mission Local she planned to resign. Her office released a statement about an "acute personal health crisis." Then, over the weekend of March 29-30, community response poured in — Calle 24, the Latino Task Force, even political rivals — urging her to stay. By March 30, the framing shifted: she's taking a medical leave of absence, not resigning. No formal resignation has been filed with the Board clerk's office.

**Why it matters for D9:** If Fielder resigns, Lurie appoints her replacement. He already leans moderate, and D9 is the Board's last reliably progressive seat. Community leaders have been explicit: "Whoever gets appointed is not going to be a Jackie." Lurie's last appointment — Beya Alcaraz for D4 — resigned after 7 days when reporters uncovered problems, so the stakes are high.

**What to watch:** Whether the leave is temporary or becomes permanent. Without Fielder's vote, progressive priorities on the Board (blocking layoffs, opposing charter reform, softening market-rate development impacts) lose a critical vote.

## Housing Pipeline: D9 Is Building

D9's streamlined housing pipeline is one of the most active in the city, with 1,100+ units across ~20 projects. The breakdown:

**Under construction or recently approved:**
- **1979 Mission St (La Maravilla)** — 134 units (Phase 1), 100% affordable. Construction began early 2026. This is the big one.
- **1515 South Van Ness (Casa Adelante)** — 168 units, 100% affordable. Under construction since April 2025, expected 2027.
- **2675 Folsom St** — 237 affordable senior housing units. Approval letter issued March 2025.
- **3333 Mission St** — 70 affordable senior units. Approved (revised design).
- **3300 Mission St** — 35 affordable units (AB 2011), approved. The former fire-damaged building at 29th/Mission.
- **34 Woodward St** — 4 units (warehouse conversion), approved November 2025.

**In the review queue:**
- **344 14th St** — 164 units (26 affordable), 11-story mixed-use. Density bonus. Under review.
- **222 Capp St** — 70 units (11 affordable), SB 423. Currently on hold.
- **1474 South Van Ness** — 9 units (1 affordable), SB 423. Historic building restoration + rear addition.
- **134-150 Porter St** — 20 units (3 affordable), merging 5 vacant lots. Under review.
- **1458 San Bruno Ave** — 231 units (29 affordable), 7-story. Under review since 2019 — a zombie?
- **2588 Mission St** — 181 units (37 affordable). On hold after the contentious 2025 Planning Commission vote.

**The pattern:** D9's pipeline skews heavily affordable — about 60% of units are 100% affordable, far more than any other district. The affordable projects are moving faster than market-rate ones because their financing is pre-assembled (MOHCD, LIHTC, state HCD loans). Market-rate projects like 2588 Mission and 344 14th St face the same citywide dead zone: approved but stuck in the financing gap.

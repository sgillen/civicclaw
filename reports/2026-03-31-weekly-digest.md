## Potential Actions

### Featured: SFMTA Engineering Hearing — Parking Changes on Broderick & Oak
**What's happening:** SFMTA proposes converting free 2-hour residential permit parking on both sides of Broderick between Fell and Oak to general metered parking (4-hour limit, 9am-6pm Mon-Sat). The adjacent block of Oak Street between Broderick and Divisadero would get the same treatment. This requires SFMTA Board approval.

**Why you'd care:** If you park on Broderick or Oak near the Panhandle, your free permit-only street parking becomes metered. The stated goal is improving turnover, but it means feeding a meter where you didn't have to before. The RPP exemption goes away.

**What you can do:** The hearing is **Friday, April 3 at 10am**. Email Kenya Wheeler (Kenya.wheeler@sfmta.com) with "Public Hearing Item 11" in the subject line. Written comments accepted before the hearing, or join online at sfmta.com/EngHearing when the item is called.

### HPC Hearing — Compton's Cafeteria Landmark Amendment (April 1)
The Historic Preservation Commission considers amending the landmark designation for Compton's Cafeteria and the site of the 1966 Compton's Cafeteria Riot (101-121 Taylor Street at Turk). Staff recommends the amendment. If you care about how landmark designations are shaped in D5, this is a low-attendance, high-impact meeting. **Wednesday, April 1 at City Hall Room 400.** Planner: P. LaValley, (628) 652-7372.

### SFMTA Hearing — RPP Area J Expansion on Stanyan (April 3)
Two parcels at 650 and 670-672 Stanyan (east side, between Page and Haight) would become eligible for Area J residential parking permits. If you live nearby and want permit access, email lulu.brien@sfmta.com. Same hearing as above: **April 3, 10am.**

## Upper Fillmore Entertainment Zone

The Board created the Upper Fillmore Entertainment Zone covering Fillmore from Jackson to Sutter, plus California and Pine between Fillmore and Steiner. This is a nightlife and entertainment overlay — expect easier permitting for bars, music venues, and late-night spots along the corridor.

## 2245 Post Street Special Use District

The Board established a new Special Use District at 2245 Post Street in the Western Addition. This is tied to a Conditional Use Authorization and Planning Code Amendment (record 2024-011443CUA/MAP/PCA). Contact: john.dacey@sfgov.org.

## Lucky Closure on Fulton

The Board passed a resolution urging Save Mart Companies to reconsider closing the Lucky Supermarket at 1750 Fulton Street (Western Addition) on September 11, 2026. Non-binding, but it's on the record.

## Housing Pipeline

**Approved:**
- **400 Divisadero** — 203 units (22 affordable), AB 2011 + density bonus. Approval letter issued. The former gas station site has been in the pipeline since 2015; the current version is an 8-story redesign.
- **880 McAllister** — 115 units, 100% affordable senior housing at Laguna/McAllister. Approval letter issued.
- **2083 Ellis** — 9 units (1 affordable), replacing a vacant SFR. Approval letter issued.
- **530-540 Turk** — 88 units (12 affordable), 11-story building. Approval letter issued.

**On hold or under review:**
- **550 O'Farrell** — 140 units, 17-story tower replacing a parking garage. Status: On Hold. This is the kind of project that turns a parking structure into housing — worth watching.
- **468 Turk** — Modified from 101 to 85 units, under review.
- **1355 Fulton** — 66 units via 8-story addition over an existing auto repair garage, under review since 2018.
- **618-630 Octavia** — 36 net new units, pending review since 2020.

**Cancelled:**
- **39 Taylor (105 Turk)** — 112 units. Cancelled after being on hold since 2022.
- **819 Ellis** — 120 units. Was demolishing a parking garage for housing. Cancelled.
- **841 Polk** — 22 units on a vacant lot. Cancelled.

Three cancellations totaling 254 units. The pipeline is losing more to cancellation than it's adding through new approvals.

## Parking & Street Changes

The April 3 SFMTA Engineering Hearing has two D5 items:

1. **Broderick/Oak metering** (Item 11a-d): Rescinding free 2-hour RPP parking on Broderick between Fell and Oak, replacing with metered 4-hour parking. Same on Oak between Broderick and Divisadero. This means paying to park where you currently don't.

2. **Stanyan RPP expansion** (Item 3): Adding 650 and 670-672 Stanyan to RPP Area J. Good news if you live there and struggle with street parking near the Haight.

Results from the SFMTA Engineering Hearings are posted Fridays at sfmta.com/EngineeringResults.

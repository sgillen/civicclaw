## Balboa Reservoir

The Board votes Tuesday on the ground lease and financing for Building A at 105 Wisteria Lane — 158 units of 100% affordable housing for very-low and low-income households, with a $29.3M city loan and a 75-year lease at $15,000/year base rent. A separate revenue note of up to $112.7M backs the construction financing. This has been in the pipeline since 2017; Tuesday's vote is the last major legislative hurdle before construction can begin.

## Housing Pipeline

Two active D11 projects worth watching: 1601 Ocean Ave (99 market-rate + 18 affordable units, seven stories, AB 2011 pathway) was recently submitted, and 5630 Mission St (52 affordable hotel-room renovations) is under review. At 2834 San Jose Ave, a 10-unit project using the fourplex density exemption plus ADUs is currently on hold.

## Citywide

**John Elberling, 1946-2026.** The TODCO founder and longtime affordable housing advocate died this week at 79. Elberling was a defining figure of San Francisco's slow-growth movement and helped preserve thousands of affordable units in SoMa over four decades. Covered in both [48 Hills](https://48hills.org/2026/04/john-elberling-advocate-for-economic-and-housing-justice-dies-at-79/) and [Mission Local](https://missionlocal.org/2026/04/john-elberling-obituary-todco-soma-san-francisco/).

**Surveillance votes at the Board Tue 4/7.** Three surveillance technology policies are up for final passage: police electronic location tracking, DPW drones with automatic license plate readers for illegal dumping, and airport TNC queue technology. A fourth (Department of Elections security cameras) goes through Rules Committee Mon 4/6.

**14 landmark designations at Land Use Mon 4/6.** All in D8/D9 — Castro, Mission, and Haight properties including the Bob Ross House, SF AIDS Foundation building, and Maud's. These add preservation constraints that make demolition or major alteration harder at each site.

**Sacks/GrowSF.** Trump AI czar David Sacks donated to Lurie-allied GrowSF and now wants his money back. GrowSF says the cash is already spent. ([SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2026/04/03/trump-czar-david-sacks-growsf-donation/))

**Asian Law Caucus at SCOTUS.** The SF-based civil rights group is at the center of the Supreme Court birthright citizenship case, with deep historical roots in the Chinese Exclusion Act era. ([SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2026/04/04/asian-law-caucus-supreme-court-birthright-citizenship/))

**Rod Diridon, 1938-2026.** The Bay Area transit leader and high-speed rail advocate died at 87. San Jose's Diridon Station bears his name. ([Streetsblog](https://sf.streetsblog.org/2026/04/04/obit-rod-diridon-transit-leader-and-high-speed-rail-advocate-dies-at-87))

# District 7 Weekly Digest — April 4, 2026

**Supervisor Myrna Melgar**

## TLDR

- **Balboa Reservoir Building A** gets its Board vote Tuesday: 158 affordable units at 105 Wisteria Lane, with a $29M city loan and 75-year ground lease. This is the first building in the long-awaited Balboa Reservoir development, in D7.
- **Melgar's Land Use Committee hears 14 landmark designations Monday** -- the biggest single batch this year, mostly in the Castro and Mission corridors. None are in D7, but Melgar chairs the hearing and controls the pace.
- **Board of Supervisors votes on surveillance technology policies Tuesday** -- police location tracking, DPW drones with license plate readers, and airport queue tech. Three separate ordinances.
- **Liquor license transfer at 77 Cambon Drive (Bark 77)** goes before Public Safety Committee Thursday -- a new on-sale liquor venue near the Lake Merced corridor.
- **250 Laguna Honda Blvd affordable housing (172 units)** received its approval letter -- a fully streamlined, 100% affordable project for very-low and low-income families.
- **SF budget picture improving**; CEO tax revenue may reduce the need for service cuts Lurie proposed. The mayor is simultaneously pushing to reduce that tax.
- **John Elberling**, a defining figure in SF affordable housing and SoMa politics, died at 79.

## Potential Actions

### Featured: Balboa Reservoir Building A -- Board Vote Tuesday

**What's happening:** The Board votes Tue 4/7 on the ground lease and $29.3M loan for Balboa Reservoir Building A (105 Wisteria Lane) -- 158 units of 100% affordable housing at 30-50% AMI, the first building in the Balboa Reservoir development. This has been in the pipeline for years.

**Why you'd care:** This is the single largest affordable housing project moving forward in D7 right now. It sets the pace for the rest of the Balboa Reservoir site. If you live near Ocean Avenue or the City College area, construction will eventually affect traffic and transit.

**What you can do:** Public comment is open at the Board meeting, Tue 4/7 at 2:00 PM, City Hall. Or email the full Board: board.of.supervisors@sfgov.org. Reference file #260242.

## Housing Pipeline

The 172-unit, 100% affordable project at **250 Laguna Honda Blvd** has its approval letter -- streamlined via AB 2162 with permanent supportive housing and large-family units at 30-50% AMI. Separately, a vacant commercial space at **519 Monterey Blvd** filed to convert to a residential unit.

## Permits

Three notable new filings: a $140K facade and interior renovation at **535 Oak Park Dr**, a rear addition and remodel at **1528 11th Ave** ($125K), and a kitchen addition at **327 Staples Ave** ($210K). A detached garage at **1601 Monterey Blvd** is converting to recreational space. The rest is roofing, bathroom remodels, and dry rot -- typical week.

## Citywide

**Budget & taxes.** City revenue is tracking better than projected. The CEO/executive pay tax (Prop L) is generating enough that proposed service cuts may not be needed. Mayor Lurie is pushing to reduce that same tax, drawing criticism that he's undermining the revenue source funding the recovery. The Sunshine Ordinance Task Force voted 8-1 that Lurie violated open-records law regarding phone records with Trump; the Ethics Commission will weigh in next.

**Surveillance policy.** Three surveillance ordinances hit the Board Tuesday: police electronic location tracking, DPW drones with automatic license plate readers for illegal dumping, and airport ride-hail queue tech. These have been working through committee since late 2025.

**Congressional race.** At this week's District 11 congressional debate, Wiener, Chan, and Chakrabarti clashed on Taiwan, Gaza, and local transit. The June 2 primary is two months away.

**John Elberling (1946-2026).** The TODCO leader and longtime SoMa affordable housing advocate died at 79. He helped preserve thousands of affordable units south of Market and was a major force in SF progressive politics for decades.

**David Sacks and GrowSF.** Trump AI czar David Sacks is trying to claw back a donation to Lurie-allied group GrowSF. GrowSF says the money is already spent.

**Trans org funding.** SF trans service providers are navigating dual threats: federal funding cuts and upcoming city budget reductions. Providers describe deep uncertainty.

**Asian Law Caucus at SCOTUS.** The SF-based civil rights group is central to the Supreme Court birthright citizenship case, with deep historical roots in the 14th Amendment.

**Rod Diridon (1938-2026).** The Bay Area transit leader and high-speed rail advocate died at 87. San Jose's Diridon Station is named for him.

**Police oversight.** A resolution affirming independent civilian oversight of the Police Commission is before the Rules Committee Monday.

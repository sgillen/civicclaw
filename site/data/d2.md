## Potential Actions

### Featured: Board votes on police surveillance policies — Tue 4/7, 2 PM

**What's happening.** The full Board takes final votes on three surveillance technology policies: (1) SFPD use of electronic location tracking devices, (2) DPW drones, and (3) DPW illegal-dumping cameras with automatic license plate readers. These have been through committee and are on unfinished business / first reading.

**Why you'd care.** These policies set the rules for how city agencies can track people and vehicles. The location-tracking and ALPR policies in particular expand what data the city collects and how long it's retained. The trade-off is public safety enforcement vs. privacy — the illegal-dumping cameras, for instance, would photograph license plates on public streets.

**What you can do.** Public comment is open at the Board meeting, Tue 4/7, 2:00 PM, City Hall Room 250. You can also email the full Board at board.of.supervisors@sfgov.org before the meeting. Reference file numbers 250979 (location tracking), 260334 (DPW drones/ALPR), and 260272 (Elections security cameras, at Rules Committee Mon 4/6).

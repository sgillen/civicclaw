## Potential Actions

### Featured: Surveillance Policy Votes — Board of Supervisors, Tue 4/7, 2 PM

**What's happening:** The Board votes on three surveillance technology policies in one week: DPW drones and automatic license plate readers for illegal dumping, police use of electronic location tracking devices, and (at Rules Monday) Elections department security cameras. That's unusual volume.

**Why you'd care:** These policies set the rules for how city agencies can track people and property. Drone surveillance and ALPR expand government monitoring capability. The police location-tracking policy determines when SFPD can use GPS and cell-site data. If you have a view on the tradeoff between enforcement and privacy, this is the moment.

**What you can do:** Public comment at Tuesday's Board meeting (2 PM, City Hall Room 250), or email the Board clerk: board.of.supervisors@sfgov.org. Rules Committee meets Monday 4/6 at 10 AM for the Elections camera policy.

## Potential Actions

### Featured: Balboa Reservoir Building A — Board Vote Tue 4/7, 2 PM

**What's happening:** The Board votes on the ground lease and $29.3M loan for Building A at Balboa Reservoir (105 Wisteria Lane) — 158 units of 100% affordable housing at 30-50% AMI. This has been in the pipeline for years.

**Why you'd care:** The single largest affordable housing project moving forward in D7. It sets the pace for the rest of Balboa Reservoir. Construction will affect traffic and transit around Ocean Avenue and City College.

**What you can do:** Public comment at the Board meeting, Tue 4/7, 2 PM, City Hall. Email: board.of.supervisors@sfgov.org. Reference file #260242.

## Citywide

**Budget & Policy.** City revenue is tracking above projections, shrinking the deficit by ~$300M. Mayor Lurie is pushing to cut the CEO/executive pay tax — the same levy that helped close the gap. The Board also votes Tuesday on $34M police overtime reappropriation and the Climate Action Plan update.

**Surveillance.** Four surveillance technology policies in one week: police electronic location tracking, DPW drones with automatic license plate readers, and airport ride-hail queue tech at the Board Tuesday; Elections dept security cameras at Rules Committee Monday.

**Housing Pipeline.** Balboa Reservoir Building A (158 affordable units) goes to the Board Tuesday for its ground lease and $29M loan — the first building to break ground at the long-planned site. The 790-unit Marina Safeway project (15 Marina Blvd) is the city's largest under review. Meanwhile, D5 lost 254 units to cancellations this cycle.

**14 Landmark Designations.** Land Use Committee hears the largest single batch this year Monday — Bob Ross House, SF AIDS Foundation, Maud's, Castro Rock Steam Baths, and 10 others, all in D8/D9. Each adds Article 10 preservation constraints.

**Ethics & Money.** Sunshine Ordinance Task Force voted 8-1 that Lurie violated open-records law over Trump phone records; the Ethics Commission takes it up next. Trump AI czar David Sacks asked Lurie-allied GrowSF to return a donation — GrowSF says it's already spent ([SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2026/04/03/trump-czar-david-sacks-growsf-donation/)).

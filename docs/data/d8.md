## Potential Actions

### Featured: 11 LGBTQ+ and Heritage Landmark Designations — Land Use Committee, Mon 4/6, 1:30 PM

**What's happening:** The Land Use and Transportation Committee hears 14 landmark designations Monday. Eleven are in D8, sponsored by Mandelman. Sites include Maud's (929 Cole St, one of the country's first lesbian bars), Castro Rock Steam Baths (578 Castro), the SF AIDS Foundation building (514 Castro), Bob Ross House (4200 20th St), Full Moon Coffeehouse (4416 18th St), Bank of Italy (400 Castro), Sha'ar Zahav synagogue (220 Danvers), American Indian Historical Society (1451 Masonic), St. Nicholas Cathedral (2005 15th St), Geilfuss on Guerrero (102 Guerrero), and Hose Company #30 (1757 Waller).

**Why you'd care:** This is the single largest batch of landmark designations in D8 in recent memory. Landmark status means Article 10 preservation constraints — demolition and major exterior alterations become much harder. Several of these sites are central to Castro/LGBTQ+ heritage. Whether you see that as overdue protection or a constraint on future development, this is the hearing to weigh in.

**What you can do:** Public comment is open at the hearing (City Hall, 1:30 PM). A 2-minute email works too: LandUse@sfgov.org. Reference the specific file numbers (260151 through 260164) if you want to comment on individual sites.

## Landmarks Deep Dive

Eleven of the 14 sites at Land Use Monday are in D8. Several tell the story of the Castro and LGBTQ+ San Francisco:

- **Maud's** (929 Cole St) — one of the first lesbian bars in the U.S.
- **Castro Rock Steam Baths** (578 Castro) — part of the Castro's LGBTQ+ social infrastructure
- **SF AIDS Foundation** (514 Castro) — epicenter of the city's HIV/AIDS response
- **Bob Ross House** (4200 20th St) — named for the LGBTQ+ activist, not the painter
- **Full Moon Coffeehouse** (4416 18th St) — community gathering space
- **Sha'ar Zahav** (220 Danvers) — LGBTQ+-affirming synagogue, first of its kind

Other D8 sites: Bank of Italy (400 Castro), American Indian Historical Society (1451 Masonic), St. Nicholas Cathedral (2005 15th St), Geilfuss on Guerrero (102 Guerrero), Hose Co #30 (1757 Waller). Three more designations on the docket are in adjacent districts.

Each designation adds Article 10 preservation constraints. If these pass committee, they go to the full Board for a vote.

## Potential Actions

### 🎯 Featured Action: SFMTA Engineering Hearing — Broderick Street Parking Meters

**Thursday, April 3, 10am** | Online at sfmta.com/EngHearing

**What's happening:** SFMTA wants to install parking meters on Broderick Street between Fell and Oak, converting the current 2-hour residential permit parking to 4-hour metered parking (9am-6pm Mon-Sat). The adjacent block of Oak Street (Broderick to Divisadero) would also get meters.

**Why you'd care:** If you park on Broderick near the Panhandle, this changes your daily calculus. Meters mean paying to park in front of your own home. The stated goal is "parking availability and turnover" — meaning faster churn for nearby businesses. Whether that trade-off works for you depends on whether you're a resident or a visitor.

**What you can do:**
- Join online when Item 11 is called, raise hand to comment
- Email kenya.wheeler@sfmta.com with "Public Hearing" in subject
- Results posted Friday at sfmta.com/EngineeringResults

| 34 Woodward (warehouse conversion) | 4 | 0 | AB 2533 | Approved 11/10 |

**The 1687 Market project is notable:** 101 units of 100% affordable housing for artists, plus a black box theater and rehearsal spaces. No vehicle parking, 110 bike spaces. 17 stories on the Market/Gough corner. This is AB 2011 working as intended — streamlined approval for 100% affordable.

**On Hold:**
- **395 3rd Street** — 522-unit tower (53 affordable) is on hold. The project increased height from 370ft to 391ft and boosted density bonus from 42.5% to 50%, but something stalled it.

**In Review:**
- **3275 San Bruno Ave** — 47 units across three buildings, State Density Bonus, D10
- **36 Otis St** — 36 affordable units (was 45,000 sf, redesigned to 41,500 sf with residential on upper floors)

**Pattern:** The streamlined pathways (AB 2011, SB 35, Density Bonus) are producing approvals. The 100% affordable projects are moving faster than market-rate.
